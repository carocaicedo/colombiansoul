Colombian Wayuu bags.

The Wayuu is a Native South American ethnic group of the Guajira Peninsula in northern Colombia and northwest Venezuela. They live in small settlements called "rancherias" which consist of five or six houses. Within these rancherias, the Wayuu people are able to preserve a way of life that has been passed down through generations and remains almost untouched by modern culture. One of the most significant aspects of culture the Wayuu women practice is the art of weaving Mochilas (bags). Each Wayuu mother teaches her daughter how to weave and crochet, keeping the tradition as alive and vibrant as ever. The making of a Wayuu bag can take between 15 and 30 days, depending on the technic and the materials, and the beautiful part of it is that the “kanas”, unique drawings never start as print patterns but they come straight from the memory and imagination of each Wayuu weaver. This means every design is exclusive and is never repeat it again.
